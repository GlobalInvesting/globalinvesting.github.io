Audience Profile

Who uses the Global Investing dashboard

The dashboard attracts an intentional audience: people who seek out fundamental data, not passive scrollers. Every page — from the scoring methodology to the COT analysis — requires active engagement.

Active Forex Traders
Retail and semi-professional traders who incorporate macro fundamentals into their process. They use the strength scores and carry rankings to qualify setups and manage directional bias across sessions.
Macro Analysts & Researchers
Financial professionals who need a fast, structured overview of G8 macro conditions — GDP, inflation, rates, COT positioning — without building their own data infrastructure.
Finance Students & CFA/CMT Candidates
Advanced students using the dashboard as a practical reference alongside academic study. The methodology documentation and educational guides make it a credible supplement to formal training.
Why this audience is valuable
01
High intent, active decision-making
Visitors arrive looking for specific data to inform real financial decisions. This is a fundamentally different mindset from entertainment or general news consumption — and it correlates directly with openness to relevant financial products and services.
02
No general-interest dilution
Every page — the scoring dashboard, carry trade analysis, COT positioning, economic calendar — requires prior knowledge to interpret. The content itself acts as a natural audience filter. There is no lifestyle or entertainment traffic.
03
Daily return frequency
Forex traders check macro conditions before each session. The dashboard is designed as a daily tool, not a one-time reference — meaning ad exposure compounds with each visit rather than relying on one-time acquisition.
04
Bilingual reach — ES + EN
The dashboard and educational guides are in Spanish; the landing page is in English. This creates a rare dual-market audience: Latin American and Spanish traders on one side, and the broader international English-speaking forex community on the other.

Ideal Partners

Who is a good fit

We prioritise partnerships with organisations whose offering is genuinely relevant to the audience. Contextual alignment — not volume — is the basis for every conversation.

Brokers & Trading Platforms
Forex and CFD brokers, prop firms, copy-trading platforms
The clearest audience fit. Visitors are already in the process of forming a directional view on a currency pair — a moment of high commercial relevance for any trading platform. Particularly suited to brokers with a fundamental or macro-oriented value proposition.
Financial Data & Tools
Data providers, charting platforms, screening tools
Traders who use fundamental dashboards also use professional charting and data tools. Complementary, non-competing products from TradingView-type platforms to Bloomberg Terminal alternative services are a natural fit for this audience.
Education & Certification
CFA, CMT, financial courses, trading academies
The educational guides on COT analysis, carry trade, central bank frameworks and the scoring methodology attract an audience already invested in structured learning. Programmes that build on this foundation — rather than starting from scratch — convert well.
Financial Institutions
Banks, asset managers, FX desks looking for brand visibility
Institutional visibility among an engaged, analytically sophisticated audience. Suitable for institutions building brand recognition in the retail-to-professional pipeline — research desks, wealth management arms or FX advisory services targeting active traders.
Editorial Independence

What we will and will not do

The analytical credibility of the dashboard is Global Investing's primary asset — and the reason this audience exists. We maintain a strict separation between editorial content and commercial relationships.

Advertisers do not influence the strength scores, the methodology, the news selection or any analytical content on the site. This is non-negotiable and disclosed to users.

Editorial policy
+ Clear "sponsored" or "partner" labelling on all paid placements
+ Contextual relevance requirement — partners must be relevant to the audience
+ Content partnerships published with full partner attribution
+ Right to decline any partnership that creates a conflict of interest
– No editorial influence over scoring, rankings or analytical content
– No undisclosed paid mentions or implied endorsements
– No partnerships with unregulated or non-compliant financial services
